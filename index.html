Diamond Facts

A diamond is a precious stone consisting of a clear and colorless crystalline form of pure carbon, the hardest naturally occurring substance.

They are the hardest Gemstones known to man and can be scratched only by other Diamonds.

For ages, diamonds have been an indication of influence, riches and status. Diamonds are rare because of it takes the combination of incredible high temperature and pressure (from 100 miles deep within the Earth) to grow them in the Earth.But what makes it truly Valuable is that this Rarity coincides with the desire of many to possess it.

Diamonds come in many sizes, shapes, colors, and with different inward qualities. Two diamonds of a similar lucidity, weight, and cut can vary in esteem dependent on shading alone. Even the slightest hint of color can make a dramatic difference in value.
